What is this?

Japanese uses two phonetic alphabets called Hiragana and Katakana. They represent the exact same sounds, but are used in different situations:

Hiragana (ひらがな) — the curvy one. Used for native Japanese words and grammar.
あ a か ka さ sa

Katakana (カタカナ) — the angular one. Used for foreign/borrowed words (e.g. コーヒー = coffee).
ア a カ ka サ sa

The table below (五十音表 = "50-sound chart") organizes all the sounds. Each row shares a consonant, each column shares a vowel. Start with Hiragana — it's what beginners learn first!
